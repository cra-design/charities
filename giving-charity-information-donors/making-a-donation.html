Making a donation

When you donate to a registered charity, you can trust that we monitor and oversee its operations to protect the integrity of the sector.

For more information, visit Regulating charities

Before you donate

Choose a registered organization

While most charities operate with integrity, there are fraudulent organizations that take advantage of donors.

Protect yourself by researching the charity you want to support before you donate:

  • List of charities and certain other qualified donees
  • Other organizations that can issue donation receipts

Find out if your donation is eligible for a tax receipt

For your donation to qualify as a gift, it must be given freely, without any form of obligation or expectation of personal benefit.

For a detailed list of transactions that generally do not qualify as gifts, visit What is a gift?

How receipts are issued

Ask the organization if they provide official donation receipts and whether any restrictions apply.

Remember that without a valid receipt, you won’t be able to claim a charitable tax credit or deduction.

A registered charity can only issue an official donation receipt to the name of the individual or organization that made the gift.

For more information: When should a charity issue a receipt?

How to donate

Registered charities have different ways of receiving donations. If you need help, contact the charity you are donating to and ask what your options are.

After you donate

Review your official donation receipts

If the charity agreed to provide you with an official donation receipt, you can expect to receive a donation receipt by February 28 of the calendar year following your donation.

Some registered charities may issue receipts throughout the year, while others may provide one cumulative receipt for cash donations at the end of the year.

If you make multiple non-cash gifts, you must get a separate receipt for each donation. The value of each non-cash gift must be its fair market value at the time of donation.

Once you receive your official donation receipt, check that all the required information has been included. The information must be readable and not easily altered.

For detailed information and examples, visit:

  • What must be included on a donation receipt
  • Sample official donation receipts

If there is an error on a donation receipt that was issued to you or if it was lost, the charity can issue a replacement receipt. For more information on getting a replacement receipt, visit Correcting or replacing official donation receipts.

Claim your charitable tax credit

If you or your spouse or common-law partner donated money or other property to a registered charity or other qualified donee, and you have an official donation receipt, you may be able to claim federal and provincial/territorial non-refundable tax credits.

Generally, you can claim part or all of the eligible amount of your gifts, up to 75% of your net income for the year.

To claim your charitable tax credit:

  1. Complete Schedule 9
  2. Use the amounts from Schedule 9 to make a claim on Line 34900 - Donations and gifts of your tax and benefit return

How to carry forward unclaimed donations

If it’s more beneficial, you can carry forward unclaimed donations to a future tax year instead of claiming them for the current year. However:

  • Donations can only be claimed once
  • Any carried-forward donations must be claimed first before new ones

You have up to 5 years (or 10 years for a gift of ecologically sensitive land made after February 10, 2014) to claim them. For more information, including advice on supporting documents, visit Completing your Schedule 9.

Contact us

For any questions, please contact the Charities Directorate. Our client service representatives can provide you with general information about registered charities and donations.
